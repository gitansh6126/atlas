# Atlas - User Documentation

Welcome to Atlas!

Atlas is your local, block-based knowledge workspace -- a fast, offline-first tool for organizing ideas, notes, projects, and everything in between. Think of it as a powerful mix between Notion and Obsidian, built to live right on your computer.

## What is Atlas?

Atlas lets you create flexible pages made of **blocks** -- building units like paragraphs, headings, lists, cards, grids, and more. You arrange these blocks however you like, and organize everything into **workspaces** for different areas of your life.

Key highlights:
- Fully offline -- your data stays on your machine
- Block-based editing -- content is modular and flexible
- Multiple workspaces -- one for work, one for personal, one for hobbies
- Light and Dark themes -- work how you like
- Full-text search -- find anything in seconds
- Rich formatting and embeds -- from paragraphs to custom HTML

## First Steps

New to Atlas? Here is the best way to start:

1. **Install Atlas** -- see the [Getting Started guide](getting-started.md)
2. **Create your first workspace** -- a private space for all your pages
3. **Make your first page** -- give it a title and add some blocks
4. **Try the `/` menu** -- press `/` anywhere to insert blocks instantly
5. **Explore blocks** -- headings, lists, cards, grids, and more

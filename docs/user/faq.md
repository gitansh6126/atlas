# FAQ

## General Questions

### What is Atlas?
Atlas is a local, block-based knowledge workspace. It lets you create pages made of blocks (like paragraphs, headings, cards, and grids) and organize them into workspaces.

### Is Atlas free?
Yes, Atlas is free to use.

### Does Atlas work offline?
Absolutely! Atlas is fully offline. All your data is stored locally on your computer.

---

## Workspaces & Pages

### How many workspaces can I have?
As many as you like. Many users create separate workspaces for work, personal, and side projects.

### Can I move a page from one workspace to another?
Currently, you can copy content and paste it into a new page in another workspace. A direct move feature is planned for the future.

### What happens if I delete a workspace?
It is permanently removed from your computer along with all its pages and files. This cannot be undone.

---

## Blocks & Editing

### What block types does Atlas support?
Atlas supports paragraphs, headings, lists, dividers, grids, cards, galleries, kanban boards, calendars, labels, tags, and HTML embeds. See the [Blocks](blocks.md) guide for details.

### Can I undo a mistake?
Yes. Press `Ctrl + Z` (or `Cmd + Z` on macOS) to undo. Use `Ctrl + Shift + Z` to redo.

### How do I insert a block?
Press `/` anywhere on the page to open the slash command menu and pick a block.

---

## Search & Export

### Does Atlas search inside all pages?
Yes, Atlas searches all page titles and content in your current workspace.

### Can I export my notes to PDF?
PDF export is a planned feature. For now, you can export pages as Markdown.

---

## Troubleshooting

### Atlas is running slowly. What should I do?
See the [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md) guide for performance tips.

### Where is my data saved?
In your workspace folder, which you chose when creating the workspace. All data is stored locally as files.

# Workspaces

Workspaces are the top-level containers in Atlas. Think of them as separate notebooks — you might have one for **work**, one for **personal**, and one for a **side project**.

## What is a Workspace?

A workspace stores:
- All your **pages**
- **Search indexes** for fast lookup
- Your **settings** (theme, sidebar mode, etc.)

---

## Creating a Workspace

1. Open Atlas and look for the **workspace selector** (top-left area)
2. Click the **+** or **New Workspace** button
3. Enter a name (e.g., `Work`, `Journal`, `My Project`)
4. Choose where on your computer to save it
5. Click **Create**

Your new workspace will open automatically.

---

## Switching Workspaces

If you have multiple workspaces:

1. Click the **Workspace Name** at the top-left
2. A dropdown list appears showing all your workspaces
3. Click the one you want to switch to

Atlas loads it instantly — no delays.

---

## Managing Workspaces

You can **rename**, **duplicate**, or **delete** a workspace.

### Rename a Workspace

1. Right-click the workspace name in the switcher
2. Select **Rename**
3. Type the new name and press **Enter**

### Delete a Workspace

⚠️ **Warning**: Deleting a workspace removes all pages inside it. This cannot be undone.

1. Right-click the workspace name
2. Select **Delete**
3. Confirm the warning dialog

---

## Workspace Best Practices

- 📁 **Use separate workspaces for separate contexts** — keep work and personal notes apart
- 📁 Name workspaces clearly so you can switch quickly
- 📁 Avoid creating too many workspaces; use **pages** and **tags** for fine-grained organization

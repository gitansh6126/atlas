# Sidebar

The **sidebar** in Atlas is your navigation hub, showing the page list, workspace switcher, and quick access tools.

## Collapsing and Expanding the Sidebar

- **Toggle button**: Click the arrow icon at the sidebar's edge to collapse or expand
- **Shortcut**: Press `Ctrl + B` (macOS: `Cmd + B`)

When collapsed, the sidebar hides to give you more room for the page editor.

---

## Resizing the Sidebar

When the sidebar is expanded, you can resize it:

1. Hover over the right edge of the sidebar
2. A **drag handle** appears (a thin vertical line)
3. Click and drag left or right to adjust the width

---

## Sidebar Modes

Atlas offers two sidebar modes to control how the sidebar behaves.

### Fixed Mode (Default)

- Width is **locked at 240px**
- Sidebar is always visible unless explicitly collapsed
- Best for: users who like a stable, predictable layout

### Variable Mode

- Width can be dragged anywhere between **180px and 480px**
- Gives more flexibility for long page titles or deep nesting
- Best for: users who want a wider or narrower sidebar

### Switching Modes

1. Right-click the sidebar header or drag handle
2. Select **Sidebar Mode**
3. Choose **Fixed** or **Variable**

---

## Sidebar Contents

- **Workspace name** — click to switch workspaces
- **+ New Page** button
- **Page tree** — your pages, nested hierarchies, and drag-to-reorder
- **Search icon** — quick access to full-text search

---

> 💡 **Tip**: Use Variable mode if you have deeply nested pages with long smokers ⁎a wider sidebar keeps titles readable.
